About Me

Hello my name is Pablo and I like to eat peanut butter and honey sandwiches. I am currently studying Electrical, Computer and Aerospace Engineering. I grew up in México City and now my family lives in St. Louis, Missouri. I enjoy going back to visit family and I love México with a passion. I spent all of my teenage years playing sports like American football, swimming, wresting, playing water polo and pick up basketball. Now I'm a full time student with limited mobility and knee pain. Jokes aside, I couldn't be more grateful for everything I've learned in the past year.

Since I was a young boy, I have always wanted to become an engineer and inventor!

What I do

I am a full time student and I work part time. I am also part of my school's microsatellite team and amateur radio club. In my free time I like to play the guitar and read.

Over the summer I worked at my school's Electromagnetic Compatibility (EMC) Lab where I had the priveledge to work on a project for the Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA). The project involved a lot of reverse engineering, learning and sleep deprivation. I also worked full time as a contractor at a company in Saint Louis, where I enhanced programs that evaluate power transformers. This was an intense summer but I didn't mind driving back and forth:)

This semester I'm helping the microsatellite team by designing, building and testing a low-noise amplifier (LNA) for one of the satellites that we hope gets launched to space next year.

I am not a web developer nor have I taken an HTML & CSS course so a lot is poorly written (but at least it's not as bad as my grammar). The great thing is that I'm good at copying and pasting haha.
